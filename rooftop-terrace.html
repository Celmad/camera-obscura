Rooftop Terrace

A wonderful floor with open space and breathtaking panoramas. Includes high powered telescopes, free to use so you can see the city in more detail!

We have an excellent 360 degree city view overlooking Edinburgh’s Old and New Towns as well as Edinburgh Castle. Informational panels will help you identify what you are looking at. It is the perfect place for taking photos of this beautiful city.

The Camera Obscura room and terrace are 160 years old and was built in 1853. Shows start every 15-20 min.

Remember to take your camera!

View North

Princes Street, Princes Street Gardens, New Town, Leith, Firth of Forth, Kingdom of Fife

Take a look at a bird’s eye view of Edinburgh’s New Town dated back to the 18th century.

The stunning Firth of Forth separates Edinburgh from the Kingdom of Fife.

Significant things to notice:

  • Royal Botanic Gardens
  • The Forth Bridges
  • Famous Jenners Department Store
  • You can even see the buses in Fife!

View East

The Royal Mile, St. Giles Cathedral, Calton Hill, Arthur’s Seat, Holyrood Palace, East Lothian Coast

The only spot in the whole city that allows you to see the view “down the mile”! The Royal Mile was used for various purposes - from markets for meetings as well as a place for public executions.

St Giles Cathedral has been Edinburgh’s principal church for nearly 800 years.

Significant things to notice:

  • Calton Hill (National Monument of Scotland, Nelson Monument etc.)
  • Royal Mile
  • St Giles Cathedral

View South

The Pentland Hills, The Royal Observatory, The Lothians

First of all, you can see some of Edinburgh’s academic buildings as well as the historic Kirk of Greyfriars. In the background, you can notice the beauty of Pentland Hills.

Significant things to notice:

  • Edinburgh’s academic buildings, including Edinburgh College of Art and the University of Edinburgh, George Heriot’s School
  • Kirk of Greyfriars
  • Pentland Hills

View West

Edinburgh Castle, Edinburgh’s West End, The Forth Bridges

The west view is the magnificent Edinburgh Castle and it’s oldest building - St Margaret’s Chapel. From here, you can also see Fettes College as well as Fountainbridge, which is on the south west and nearby there is also McEwan Brewery.

Significant things to notice:

  • Edinburgh Castle
  • St Margaret’s Chapel
  • Fettes College
  • The area of Fountainbridge
